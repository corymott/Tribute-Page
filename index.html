Mahatma Ghandi

The man who freed India

Here is a timeline of Ghandi's life

  • 1869 - Ghandi is born in Porbandar, India.
  • 1882 - Ghandi is married to his wife Kasturbai at age 13.
  • 1888 - Ghandi's son, Harilal, is born.
  • 1888-1891 - Ghandi studies law in London. During his time in London, he meets many notable figures such as George Benard Shaw and Annie Besant.
  • 1893-1894 - Ghandi begins work at an Indian law firm in South Africa. While working there, he was exposed to heavy racial discrimination. In 1894, Ghandi founds the Natal Indian Congress to fight for Indian rights. During this time, he begins to draw attention to Indian oppression through various media outlets as well as through the ruling government.
  • 1906-1915 - In 1906, a law was passed in the Transvaal region of South Africa which forced all Indians to register with the provincial governemnt or face severe punishment. Ghandi organized a protest against the new law in which they would all face the punishment together. This became known as satyagraha. This would become a major part of Ghandi's life. Thousands of Indians were punished during these protests.
  • 1919-1924 - In 1919, Ghandi becomes a leader in the Indian National Congress political party, allowing him to further his influence over the Indian people. He then launched a noncooperation campaign against the United Kingdom, encouraging the Indian people to rely less on United Kingdom goods and create as much as they can themselves.
  • 1931 - Ghandi leads thousands of Indians on a 240-mile march to the ocean to collect their own salt. The marches led to 60,000 people being arrested. Ghandi signs an agreement with the British government to end the era of civil disobedience between the Indians and the British governemnt.
  • 1932 - Ghandi is imprisoned and, in an act of protest against Britain's segregation of the untouchables, decides to fast and causes a major upheavel in the country.
  • 1942-1944 - A retired Ghandi becomes politically active again during World War II. He worked for Indian independence in return for the help India provided during the war.
  • 1947 - India formally gains independence from the United Kingdom. Pakistan not being included into India greatly disappointed Ghandi.
  • 1948 - Ghandi passes away after being attacked on his way to prayer. His killer was a young Hindu fanatic who was upset at Ghandi's attempts to reconcile Hindus and Muslims.
